## 1. Brand Voice

### Voice Archetype: The Stylish Best Friend

Confident but never condescending. Trendy but accessible. Speaks with the assurance of someone who knows fashion, but always makes you feel like you already have great taste — you just need a little nudge.

### Personality Traits

| Trait | We Are | We Are Not |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **Confident** | Assured, decisive, "this is the one" | Arrogant, dismissive of other styles |
| **Friendly** | Warm, encouraging, inclusive | Patronizing, condescending |
| **Stylish** | Trend-aware, visually sharp | Snobby, exclusionary |
| **Helpful** | Solution-focused, actionable | Overwhelming with too many options |
| **Modern** | Current, fresh, dynamic | Tryhard, using outdated slang |

### Tone Dimensions (1–5)

- Formal ↔ Casual: **2** (casual, conversational)
- Serious ↔ Playful: **3.5** (lean playful, but not silly)
- Reserved ↔ Expressive: **4** (enthusiastic about style)
- Modern ↔ Timeless: **5** (very modern, current)

### Voice Rules

- Short, punchy sentences. Lead with the benefit.
- Second person ("you," "your style") — never "our users" or "customers."
- Active voice. Present tense.
- Fashion terms used naturally, never gatekeeping.
- Confidence-boosting — never shame, always empower.
- Inclusive language — all body types, genders, budgets.
- One CTA per section — don't overwhelm.

### We Sound Like

- "Your next great outfit is one click away."
- "Not sure what to wear? We've got you."
- "Effortless style starts here."
- "Dress for the vibe, not just the occasion."

### We Don't Sound Like

- "Our premium styling solutions leverage AI synergy..."
- "For the discerning luxury fashion consumer..."
- "Don't look basic — upgrade now!"

### Words to Use

style, vibe, look, outfit, curated, effortless, fresh, chic, polished, put-together, mood, occasion, everyday, versatile, mix-and-match, elevated, clean

### Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| cheap | Negative connotation |
| basic | Dismissive |
| boring | Judgmental |
| complicated | Undermines simplicity promise |
| luxury | Too exclusive for our audience |
| synergy | Corporate jargon |
| leverage | Corporate jargon |
| revolutionary | Overused |
| best-in-class | Vague claim |

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Hero / Headlines | Bold, inviting | "Pick the Perfect Outfit for Any Day" |
| Features | Clear, helpful | "Choose your occasion, pick your vibe, get styled." |
| CTAs | Confident, action-oriented | "Build My Outfit" / "Explore Looks" |
| Testimonials | Warm, authentic | "See what our community is wearing" |
| FAQ | Friendly, direct | "How does it work? It's simple..." |
| Error states | Encouraging | "No outfits matched — try a different vibe!" |

## 2. Messaging Framework

### Mission

We help people look and feel their best by making outfit decisions simple, fast, and inspiring.

### Vision

A world where getting dressed is the best part of your morning.

### Tagline (≤7 words)

**"Your style, simplified."**

### Value Proposition (≤20 words)

Pick your occasion, mood, and weather — and get curated outfit ideas that look fresh and feel effortless.

### Positioning Statement

Pick My Outfit is the style companion for anyone who wants to look great without overthinking it — because your perfect outfit is already waiting.

### Key Phrases

1. "Your style, simplified."
2. "Dress for the vibe."
3. "Effortless looks, every day."
4. "Curated just for you."
5. "Style that fits your life."

### Elevator Pitches

**10-second:** Pick My Outfit helps you find the perfect outfit for any occasion, mood, or weather — in seconds.

**30-second:** Ever stand in front of your closet with no idea what to wear? Pick My Outfit takes the guesswork out of getting dressed. Tell us the occasion, your mood, and the weather — and we serve up curated outfit ideas that look fresh and feel like you.

## 4. Content Rules

1. **Second person always** — "you," "your style," never "our users"
2. **Active voice** — "Pick your look" not "A look will be picked for you"
3. **Short sentences** — If it needs a semicolon, split it
4. **One CTA per section** — Don't overwhelm with choices
5. **Show, don't tell** — Use outfit imagery; copy supports, not replaces
6. **Inclusive** — All body types, all genders, all budgets
