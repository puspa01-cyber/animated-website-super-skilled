==============================================================
SITE: Château Barkalot
==============================================================

ROLE / CONTEXT
--------------
Role: You are Chief Creative Director for Pixar 3D Movies.
Context: You are making the film "King Arthur and The Knights of Barkalot."
Barkalot is a "doggie Camelot" and all the characters of Camelot are Dogs.
The film features a "Doggie Cafe" called Château Barkalot. You, the Chief
Creative Director, have commissioned a highly talented Web Designer, Jeremy,
to create the Château Barkalot website.

Assign each knight a different dog breed of your choice. Render every
character in clean Pixar 3D cartoon style — soft subsurface scattering,
painterly lighting, expressive oversized eyes, stylized fur shader.

Characters:
- King Arthur – the leader (not always counted as a knight, but often included)
- Sir Lancelot – greatest warrior, tragic love with Guinevere
- Sir Galahad – pure knight, finds the Holy Grail
- Sir Gawain – loyal and noble (Green Knight story)
- Sir Percival – Grail seeker
- Sir Tristan – tragic romance with Isolde
- Sir Bedivere – returns Excalibur to the lake
- Sir Kay – Arthur's foster brother
- Sir Bors – one of the Grail achievers
- Sir Mordred – traitor, Arthur's downfall
- Sir Gareth – humble, noble knight
- Sir Agravain – exposes Lancelot/Guinevere
- Sir Gaheris – Gawain's brother
- Sir Palamedes – Saracen knight
- Sir Ector – Arthur's foster father (sometimes included)
- Sir Lamorak – one of the strongest knights
- Merlin – The Wizard, chief advisor to Arthur
- Lady Guinevere


BUILD STACK
-----------
1. Stitch (layout generation)
2. UI UX Pro Max (style / palette / typography selection)
3. Firecrawl (reference scrape)
4. Magic / 21st.dev (marketing blocks)
5. Nano Banana 2 (hero + feature imagery)
6. Brand skill (voice + messaging)
7. Design skill (logo, icons, OG image)
8. Design System skill (token architecture)
9. UI Styling skill (shadcn/ui interactive components)


PURPOSE
-------
A dog café serving coffee, light food, and a welcoming space where doggies
relax with other doggies, attend small events, and meet adoptable pet doggies.


==============================================================
STEP 2 — BRAND VOICE (for `brand` skill)
==============================================================

Voice archetype: The Jester-Sage — Arthurian whimsy meets modern neighborhood
warmth. Speaks like a kindly royal herald who has clearly spent the morning
giving belly rubs.

Tone dimensions (scale 1–5):
- Formal ↔ Casual:        2 (casual with courtly flourishes)
- Serious ↔ Playful:      5 (playful)
- Reserved ↔ Expressive:  4 (expressive)
- Modern ↔ Timeless:      4 (timeless wink)

Voice rules:
- Sprinkle medieval vocabulary sparingly: "hark," "verily," "pull up a
  tankard," "the round table awaits." Never go full Ren-faire — 1 medieval
  phrase per paragraph maximum.
- Dog puns are earned, not stacked. Max one pun per section.
- Second person ("you," "your pup") — never corporate "customers" or "guests."
- Active voice. Short sentences. Warmth over cleverness when they conflict.

Words to use:   hark, noble, quest, round table, tankard, realm, pack, pup,
                paw-worthy, wag, cozy, gather
Words to avoid: leverage, synergy, solutions, premium, luxury, world-class,
                "fur babies" (too saccharine), "doggos" outside brief's own usage

Messaging framework:
- Tagline (≤7 words):        "Where every pup is knighted."
- Value prop (≤20 words):    "A cozy Camelot for coffee, pastries, and the
                              noblest pack of dogs in the realm."
- Key phrases (3–5):          "The round table awaits," "Hark — coffee is
                              served," "Every pup, a knight," "Pull up a
                              tankard," "Gather the pack"


==============================================================
STEP 3 — DESIGN SYSTEM (for `ui-ux-pro-max` + `design-system`)
==============================================================

Style keywords:   warm, playful, cozy, modern, friendly, inviting, clean,
                  Pixar 3D, soft rounded, storybook-illustrative

Preferred style direction: soft claymorphism with painterly storybook
accents — generous corner radii, tactile shadows, no sharp geometric
brutalism. Hero section may break grid with layered illustration.

COLOR SYSTEM (semantic roles — let ui-ux-pro-max pick the exact 161-palette
entries that match these roles):
- Primary:         warm sunset red/coral (castle banners)
- Secondary:       deep royal purple (twilight sky)
- Tertiary:        forest green (meadow / tabard)
- Accent:          warm golden honey (candlelight, coffee crema)
- Surface-base:    cream / parchment off-white
- Surface-raised:  soft warm beige
- Ink:             deep espresso brown (NOT pure black)
- Success/info:    sage green / sky blue from the palette

Contrast requirement: WCAG AA minimum, AAA preferred for body copy.

TYPOGRAPHY (let ui-ux-pro-max pick from the 57 pairings, matching this
direction):
- Display:   a rounded medieval-adjacent serif or storybook display (NOT
             blackletter — readable and friendly)
- Body:      humanist sans-serif, high x-height, 16–18px base
- Accent:    a single italic or hand-drawn style for pull-quotes only
- Type scale: 1.250 (major third) modular scale
- Line height: 1.55 body, 1.15 display

SPACING & LAYOUT:
- Spacing scale (4px base): 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128
- Container max-width: 1280px
- Grid: 12-col desktop, 8-col tablet, 4-col mobile
- Section vertical rhythm: 96px desktop / 64px tablet / 48px mobile

RADII:
- sm 8px  |  md 16px  |  lg 24px  |  xl 32px  |  pill 9999px
Default card radius: lg. Buttons: pill.

ELEVATION (soft claymorphism — no hard drop shadows):
- sm  0 2px 8px   rgba(espresso / 0.08)
- md  0 8px 24px  rgba(espresso / 0.10)
- lg  0 16px 48px rgba(espresso / 0.12)

MOTION LANGUAGE:
- Easing:     cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1) for entrances (soft over-settle)
- Durations:  micro 150ms, standard 300ms, expressive 600ms, hero 900ms
- Stagger:    80ms between siblings on scroll-reveal
- Hover:      CTA lift 2px + shadow md → lg, 200ms
- Hero:       title fade+rise 24px over 900ms, 120ms per-word stagger
- prefers-reduced-motion: all transform/opacity transitions collapse to
  opacity only at 200ms

==============================================================
STEP 5 — SECTIONS & LAYOUT (for Stitch)
==============================================================
Section order and goal:

1. Hero         — brand, tagline, 2 CTAs ("Reserve a Table" / "Meet the Pack")
2. About        — origin story of Château Barkalot, founder note, vibe photos
3. Menu         — Saxon-inspired brews (mead-coffee, ale-latte), pastries,
                  dog treats; 3-col card grid with price + dietary icons
4. Dog Play Area — amenities list with themed icons, capacity, rules, booking
5. Events       — calendar/list hybrid: jousting nights, adoption fairs,
                  "squire classes" (puppy training)
6. Adoptable    — portrait grid of rescue dogs, filter by size/energy, modal
                  for each with story + adopt CTA
7. Gallery      — Pinterest-style masonry of customer + pup photos
8. Testimonials — carousel, 5-star ratings, guest + pup names
9. FAQ          — accordion, 8–10 questions (dogs-per-table? off-leash? etc.)
10. Contact     — form (name, email, message, topic select), hours, map,
                  social links

Hero 21st.dev prompt:
  "A warm and playful hero section for a dog café with a large welcoming
  headline, short subtitle, two CTA buttons, cozy café atmosphere, soft
  natural lighting, rounded elements, and subtle animated accents that feel
  friendly and modern."


==============================================================
STEP 6 — IMAGERY (for Nano Banana 2, with fallback tiers)
==============================================================
Global art direction: Pixar 3D cartoon, soft subsurface scattering, painterly
golden-hour lighting, shallow depth of field, no text in images, no humans
with visible logos, consistent warm palette across all renders.

Fallback art direction (if Nano Banana 2 is unavailable and the pipeline drops
to the `design` skill, hand-authored SVG, or a third-party SVG library): the
Pixar 3D / painterly storybook direction STILL APPLIES. Do not ship flat
corporate vector art. Fallback illustrations must stay rounded, friendly,
warm-lit, and palette-bound to Step 3 tokens. Register may shift from
photographic-real (Tier 1) to stylized illustration (Tiers 2–4) — that's
acceptable, but the brand warmth must survive.

Third-party SVG (Tier 4 — undraw.co / Storyset / Blush only): royalty-free
license required. CC0 preferred; CC-BY acceptable if attribution lands in
the footer credits line. No paid, restricted, or ambiguously-licensed assets.

Aspect ratios:
- Hero:        16:9 (2560×1440 export)
- Feature:     4:3
- Portrait:    3:4 (Adoptable dogs, Knight character cards)
- Gallery:     mixed (square + 4:5)
- OG/social:   1200×630

Per-image prompts:
- Hero         — cozy dog café interior: happy Pixar-style dogs lounging
                 beside human guests sipping coffee, warm sunlight through
                 leaded windows, modern rustic decor with subtle Camelot
                 banners, shallow DoF
- About        — Pixar-style barista smiling behind a wood-and-stone counter,
                 a golden retriever sitting nearby wearing a small tabard,
                 friendly neighborhood atmosphere, soft window light
- Menu         — top-down close-up of coffee drinks inspired by Saxon
                 beverages (mead, ale) served in pewter tankards, pastries,
                 a dog-treat sampler board on a worn oak café table,
                 candlelight
- Dog Play Area — several small and medium Pixar-style dogs playing in a
                 clean indoor lounge: some miniature jousting on rocking-
                 horse mounts, others mock sword-fighting with foam swords,
                 soft textures, skylight
- Adoptable    — cheerful portrait lineup of 6 adorable Pixar-style rescue
                 dogs wearing colorful bandanas, bright backdrop, even
                 lighting, consistent 3:4 framing
- Gallery      — candid moments: customers petting dogs, sipping lattes in
                 tankards, a knight-dog receiving a pastry, relaxed social
                 space
- Knight cards — 18 portrait renders, one per character listed above, each
                 in a different dog breed, wearing character-appropriate
                 Camelot attire, consistent 3:4 framing and palette

Knight-card consistency rules (all 18 must share):
- Framing:    3:4 portrait, chest-up, subject centered, 10% breathing room
              at top and bottom.
- Lighting:   same golden-hour key light from camera-left at ~30°, soft
              warm fill from camera-right, subtle rim light on fur edges.
              No high-contrast or noir lighting on any single knight.
              Background blur matches across all 18.
- Palette:    pulls from Step 3 tokens; character-specific accent allowed
              (Lancelot red, Galahad white-gold, Mordred deep purple,
              Gawain green) but never overrides the global warm palette.
- Pose:       three-quarter view, eye-line to camera, friendly expression.
              Villains (Mordred, Agravain) may be sterner but never
              menacing — this is Pixar, not Grimdark.
- Breed:      one unique breed per knight, Chief Creative Director's
              choice. No breed repeats across the 18.
- Attire:     character-appropriate Camelot (tabard, cape, circlet,
              mantle). Subtle wear/patina on armor where it fits lore —
              Arthur pristine, Bedivere slightly worn, etc.
- Naming:     files as `knight-<name>.png` (lowercase, hyphenated) for
              downstream reference from the Knights card grid.

==============================================================
STEP 9 — ASSEMBLE & ANIMATE (final index.html)
==============================================================

RESPONSIVE BREAKPOINTS: 640 (sm), 1024 (lg), 1280 (xl). Mobile-first.

PERFORMANCE BUDGET:
- LCP  < 2.5s
- CLS  < 0.05
- Lazy-load below-the-fold imagery (loading="lazy", decoding="async").
- Use srcset for hero and feature images.

SEO / META:
- <title>Château Barkalot — Where Every Pup Is Knighted</title>
- meta description: value prop from Step 2 (≤155 chars)
- OG tags: og:title, og:description, og:image (Step 7 card), og:type=website
- Twitter card: summary_large_image
- JSON-LD: LocalBusiness / CafeOrCoffeeShop schema with address + hours

ACCESSIBILITY:
- Landmark regions: header, nav, main, footer, section[aria-label]
- Skip-to-content link
- Alt text on every image (descriptive, not decorative placeholders)
- prefers-reduced-motion honored (see Step 3 motion rules)
- Tap targets ≥ 44×44px on mobile
- Form labels always visible (no placeholder-only labels)

ANIMATION ON ASSEMBLY:
- Hero:              title fade+rise 900ms, per-word 120ms stagger
- Section reveals:   IntersectionObserver, threshold 0.15, once:true
- CTA hover:         lift 2px + shadow md→lg, 200ms
- Card hover:        lift 4px, tilt 1°, image zoom 1.03
- Menu tab change:   crossfade 200ms
- Accordion:         height transition 280ms + icon rotate 200ms
- Carousel:          slide 500ms with easing from Step 3
